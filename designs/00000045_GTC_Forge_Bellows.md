# Walipa Design #45: GTC Forge Bellows (The Atmospheric Lung)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Thermal Induction & Atmospheric Control)

## 1. The Vision
In the traditional forge, the bellows are a crude mechanical lung. In the Walipa Forge, the bellows are a precision atmospheric regulator. This design uses a series of high-static pressure induction fans (powered by the G-Bike’s 12v system) to "breathe" specifically filtered gases into the Sodium-Silicate Kiln. 

## 2. Technical Logic
The bellows interface with your Liquid Glass-treated tubes at the base of the kiln. By switching between a standard oxygen-rich intake and a CO2 "Reduction" intake (sourced from a vinegar/bicarbonate reaction), the steward can instantly shift the kiln's internal chemistry. This allows for the precise transition from oxidation to reduction environments required for fuming ionic silver and gold onto glass flutes and lenses.

## 3. Organic Form
The housing is a bio-geometric shell made of hemp-silicate masonry, shaped like the expanded ribcage of a swan. It is designed to dampen the "grit" of mechanical fan noise, replacing it with a low, meditative hum that synchronizes with the steward’s own breathing during the alchemical process.
