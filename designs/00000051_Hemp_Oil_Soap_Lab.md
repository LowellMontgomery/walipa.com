# Walipa Design #51: Hemp-Oil Soap Lab
**Status:** Phase 1 (Saponification & Transmutation)

## 1. The Vision
Hygiene in the Walipa community is an act of "Clearing the Grit." This design moves away from industrial surfactants and endocrine disruptors. We use the "Mother Tincture" logic to create a cleanser that not only removes physical dirt but also neutralizes the residual frequency of the old 3D reality.

## 2. Technical Logic
The lab utilizes a series of fumed borosilicate reaction vessels. Cold-pressed hemp oil (from Design #38) is mixed with a lye solution derived from the wood ash of the Sodium-Silicate Kiln. The reaction is "Heart-Potentized" by the steward. We add a fraction of a teaspoon of colloidal graphite to the final cure; this creates a mildly conductive soap that helps "ground" the human body during the washing process, releasing static electromagnetic buildup.

## 3. Organic Form
The lab is housed in a translucent ETFE "Clean Room." The soap itself is cured in molds shaped like river stones, vitrified on the bottom with a thin layer of liquid glass to prevent sticking. The final product has a pearlescent sheen, mirroring the "Mother of Pearl" aesthetic of the Forge.
