# Walipa Design #54: Aqueous Heat Pump (The Earth-Lung)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Geothermal Stabilization)

## 1. The Vision
To heat or cool a space usually requires "Grit"—noisy compressors and toxic refrigerants. The Walipa Heat Pump uses the constant 55°F (13°C) temperature of the earth a few feet below your garden. It is a passive "breathing" system that stabilizes the Subterranean Solarium and the Portable Shelters.

## 2. Technical Logic
This design uses a closed-loop of **ETFE Tubing** filled with a high-salinity Aqueous Humor. This loop is buried in a "Vortex" pattern (following your chaos-stirring logic) 4–6 feet underground. A small 12v pump (G-Bike compatible) moves the humor between the earth and an interior heat-exchanger made of vitrified basalt fins. It leverages the high thermal capacity of water to move "Coolth" in summer and "Warmth" in winter.

## 3. Organic Form
The interior heat-exchanger is shaped like a giant fungal gill. As air passes over the vitrified fins, it is not only tempered but also filtered of dust, mimicking the way the Nevada City canal trickle cools the surrounding forest air.
