# Walipa Design #57: Aqueous Level Sensor
**Status:** Phase 1 (Non-Electronic Fluid Monitoring)

## 1. The Vision
In the Nevada City garden, water flow is the heartbeat of the system. This design eliminates the need for 3D electronics or batteries to monitor tank levels. It uses the "Logic of the Trickle," allowing the steward to know the state of their reservoirs through purely mechanical and visual resonance.

## 2. Technical Logic
A vertical tube of borosilicate glass is connected to the base of the water tank. Inside, a buoyant "Aqueous Float" made of vitrified hemp-silicate (Design #55) rises and falls. The float is fumed with a thin layer of silver (Design #36) to make it visible from a distance. The pressure differential in the tube provides an instant, "Analog" readout of the system's potential energy.

## 3. Organic Form
The sensor is designed to look like a "Reed" growing alongside the tank. It is encased in a protective ETFE shroud to prevent the "Grit" of algae or dust from interfering with the float. It serves as a visual reminder that the community's resource flow is always transparent and balanced.
