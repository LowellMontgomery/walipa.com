# Walipa Design #61: SWOT Gravity Buoy (Oceanic/Canal)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Localized Altimetry & Flow Mapping)

## 1. The Vision
To heal the planet, we must "Listen" to the water. This buoy is a localized, low-cost version of the SWOT satellite's mission. It recognizes that every "trickle" in the Nevada City canal or every wave in the Pacific has a "Gravity Signature." By mapping these signatures, the Walipa community can predict seismic shifts, water table changes, and "Turning Point" energetic waves.

## 2. Technical Logic
The buoy uses an internal **Aqueous-Humor Lens** (Design #06) as a liquid-level sensor. As the buoy floats, it measures the micro-inclination of the water surface. This data is transmitted via the "Magnetic Breakaway" USB-C interface to the Walipa Mesh Network. It is shielded with a **Vitrified Basalt Shell** and weighted with a graphite-zinc "Grounding Rod" to prevent interference from 3D electronic noise.

## 3. Organic Form
The buoy is shaped like an "Inverted Seed Pod" or a "Lotus Bud." It is designed to be self-righting and virtually indestructible. In the Nevada City canal, it serves as a "Guardian of the Flow," signaling the health of the watershed through a pulse of light that reflects the purity and conductivity of the water.
