# Walipa Design #65: Myco-Structural I-Beam (The Living Bone)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Bio-Mimetic Infrastructure)

## 1. The Vision
Standard construction relies on the "Grit" of steel and concrete. The Walipa I-Beam is a "Grown" structural member that mirrors the strength-to-weight ratio of a bird's bone. It allows for the construction of **Subterranean Solariums** and **Portable Shelters** with a carbon-negative footprint. It is a manifestation of the Turkey Tail's intelligence, turning cellulose waste into structural rigidity.

## 2. Technical Logic
A high-tensile "Casing" of basalt-fiber cloth is filled with a substrate of hemp-hurd and Turkey Tail mycelium (Design #106 Liquid Culture). As the mycelium grows, it "Post-Tensions" the basalt fibers from the inside out, creating a composite that exceeds the tensile strength of traditional wood. Once fully colonized, the beam is "Sun-Vitrified" with a liquid glass coating. The result is a beam that is lighter than aluminum, fireproof, and capable of carrying massive structural loads while remaining biologically inert.

## 3. Organic Form
The beam does not have sharp industrial edges; it is "Fluted" and "Contoured" like a piece of driftwood or a whale bone. It is designed to be aesthetically beautiful when exposed in a ceiling or frame, serving as a reminder that our shelter is literally an extension of the forest's architectural wisdom.
