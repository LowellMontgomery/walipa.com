# Walipa Design #69: GTC Laundry System (The Thermal Rinse)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Thermal Energy Recovery & Textile Stewardship)

## 1. The Vision
In the old reality, laundry is a drain on the grid and a source of micro-plastic "grit" in the water. The Walipa Laundry System is a "Low-Friction" process that uses the residual heat of the Graphite Thermal Capacitor (GTC) to clean and dry the community’s hemp and basalt textiles. It honors the "Phoebe Truth" by recognizing that heat is a gift to be cycled, not vented.

## 2. Technical Logic
A twin-tub borosilicate or vitrified basalt basin is positioned adjacent to the GTC Battery. Water is pre-heated via an Aqueous Heat Exchanger (Design #89). The "Wash" cycle utilizes the ultrasonic frequencies of a fumed-silver resonator (Design #97 logic) to shake "grit" loose from the fibers without harsh agitation. Drying occurs in an ETFE-lined cabinet that captures the infrared signature of the GTC core, pulling moisture through a Myco-Filter to reclaim the distilled water for the next cycle.

## 3. Organic Form
The system is a sleek, black-graphite station integrated into the Forge wall. It operates in near-silence, with the only sound being the soft "thrum" of the ultrasonic resonator. The drying cabinet is translucent, allowing the steward to see the golden infrared light warming the "Mother of Pearl" hemp garments within.
