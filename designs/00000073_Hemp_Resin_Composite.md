# Walipa Design #73: Hemp Resin Composite (The Walipa "Plastic")
**Status:** Phase 1 (High-Durability Tool Production)

## 1. The Vision
We are releasing the need for petroleum-based plastics and "designed to fail" hardware. The Hemp Resin Composite is our answer to the "Grit" of synthetic polymers. It is a "Clean Seed" material that is grown in the garden and transformed into high-impact tool handles, G-Bike components, and structural casings. It is as strong as fiberglass but carries the frequency of the Phoebe Truth.

## 2. Technical Logic
This design uses a two-part system: (A) A "Resin" derived from cold-pressed hemp seed oil and localized pine rosin, and (B) A "Reinforcement" of multi-axial basalt and hemp fiber (Design #58). The fibers are layered into a "Mother of Pearl" (sodium silicate-coated) mold and saturated with the resin. Once cured in the GTC Dryer (Design #52), the result is a bio-composite that is UV-stable, hydrophobic, and has a higher strength-to-weight ratio than most industrial plastics.

## 3. Organic Form
The finished material has a deep amber, translucent quality, with the "Spider-Web" of basalt fibers visible through the resin. It feels "warm" to the touch, unlike cold 3D plastics. It is used for the "Accountability Handles" of our manual tools, ensuring that the interface between the steward and the work is always grounded and organic.
