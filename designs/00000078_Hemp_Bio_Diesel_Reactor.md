# Walipa Design #78: Hemp Bio-Diesel Reactor (The Transmuter)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Small-Scale Bio-Fuel Production)

## 1. The Vision
While we transition to the G-Bike and GTC systems, we recognize the "Grit" of existing 3D diesel infrastructure. The Bio-Diesel Reactor is a "Restorative Bridge." It allows the steward to convert the oil from hemp seeds into a clean-burning fuel that can power legacy machinery or backup generators, ensuring that no part of the harvest is "waste."

## 2. Technical Logic
This is a small-batch transesterification unit housed in a vitrified basalt vessel. Cold-pressed hemp oil (Design #38) is mixed with a catalyst (wood-ash lye) and a small amount of bio-ethanol (Design #75). The GTC Thermal Stove (Design #79) provides the gentle 55°C (131°F) required for the reaction. The resulting glycerine is reclaimed for use in Aqueous Diffusers (Design #60), while the bio-diesel is washed through a Biochar Filter (Design #43) to remove all residual grit.

## 3. Organic Form
The reactor is a "Silver-Fumed" borosilicate cylinder with a "Mother of Pearl" base. It is designed to be a transparent process, allowing the steward to see the separation of the "Gold" (fuel) from the "Grit" (residue). It is a temporary tool, a "Covenant" with the machines of the past as we move toward a fuel-less future.
