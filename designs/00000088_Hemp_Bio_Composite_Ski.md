# Walipa Design #88: Hemp Bio-Composite Ski/Sled (The Winter Glide)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Cold-Weather Mobility & Surface Stewardship)

## 1. The Vision
To move through the Nevada City winters without the "Grit" of internal combustion, we need a tool that "Glides" over the snow. The Bio-Composite Ski is a high-performance winter runner made from the very plants we grow. It is a "Low-Friction" manifestation of the Phoebe Truth, allowing the steward to navigate the terrain with the same "Laminar Flow" as the water in the canal.

## 2. Technical Logic
The core is a multi-layered "Sandwich" of **Hemp-Fiber Insulation** (Design #68) and **Basalt-Fiber Cloth**, saturated with the **Hemp Resin Composite** (Design #73). The base is treated with a thick layer of **Liquid Glass** and "Mother of Pearl" graphite fuming. This creates a "Zero-Wax" surface that is naturally hydrophobic and harder than steel, allowing for effortless movement across ice and slush without environmental degradation.

## 3. Organic Form
The skis are long, tapering "Leaves" with a slight amber translucency. They are fitted with "Magnetic Breakaway" bindings (Design #32) that interface with the steward's Myco-Leather boots. They are virtually unbreakable and designed to be "Sun-Charged" in the Subterranean Solarium, storing a minor thermal signature to prevent ice-buildup on the top surface.
