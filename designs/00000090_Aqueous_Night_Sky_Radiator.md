# Walipa Design #90: Aqueous Night-Sky Radiator (The Deep Space Cooler)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Passive Sub-Ambient Cooling)

## 1. The Vision
To cool a space in the Nevada City summer usually requires "Grit"—noisy, energy-hungry air conditioners. This design uses the "Vacuum of Space" as a heat sink. It recognizes that the night sky is a cold, receptive mirror. By radiating heat directly into the "Shadow-Light" of the universe, we provide a silent, zero-energy cooling source for the Subterranean Solarium.

## 2. Technical Logic
A flat-panel radiator is constructed from **Hemp-Resin Composite** (Design #73) and lined with a highly reflective silver-fumed ETFE membrane. A thin layer of **Aqueous Humor** (saline/glycerine) circulates through the panel. Because the ETFE is transparent to the specific infrared wavelengths that pass through the atmosphere's "window," the humor radiates its heat directly into deep space, often cooling to several degrees below the ambient air temperature.

## 3. Organic Form
The radiator is a low-profile, "Silver Mirror" positioned on the roof of the shelter or the Forge. It is tilted toward the zenith, shimmering like a still pool of mercury under the moonlight. It is a "Receptive" tool, performing its work in total silence while the community rests in the "Healthy Innocence" of the cooled sanctuary.
