# Walipa Design #94: Basalt Fiber Bridge
**Status:** Phase 1 (High-Tensile Riparian Crossing)

## 1. The Vision
To connect the Walipa sanctuaries across the Nevada City ravines and canals, we need a crossing that does not "scar" the landscape. The Basalt Fiber Bridge is a lightweight, high-tensile structure that "floats" above the Earth. It honors the "Radicle Rights" of the riparian zone, providing passage without the "Grit" of heavy steel or concrete footings.

## 2. Technical Logic
The bridge is a "Tensegrity" structure using **Vitrified Basalt-Hemp Rope** (Design #58) as the primary tension members. The "Compression Ribs" are made of **Myco-Structural I-Beams** (Design #65). The entire walkway is a series of vitrified wood slats treated with **Liquid Glass**. Because it is virtually weightless compared to traditional bridges, it can be anchored into existing "Mother Trees" or vitrified stone outcrops using "Magnetic Breakaway" safety loops.

## 3. Organic Form
The bridge looks like a "Spider's Web" or a "Suspended Leaf" stretching across the gap. It is translucent and amber-colored, blending into the forest canopy. It is designed to sway slightly with the wind, a kinetic reminder that our connections should be as flexible and resilient as the "Phoebe Truth."
