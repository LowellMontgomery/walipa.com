# Walipa Design #101: Aqueous Gimbal Mount
**Status:** Phase 1 (Zero-Friction Precision Alignment)

## 1. The Vision
To track the "True Light" of the stars or the movement of the Ark Blimps, our sensors (Design #50) must move with "Laminar Flow." Standard mechanical gimbals have "Grit" and "Stiction." The Aqueous Gimbal is a frictionless fluid-bearing mount that allows the heaviest telescopes or mirrors to be moved with the touch of a single finger. It is the mechanical manifestation of "Grace."

## 2. Technical Logic
The mount consists of two vitrified basalt hemispheres separated by a thin "film" of high-viscosity **Aqueous Humor** (saline/glycerine). The humor is pressurized via a small **Aqueous Micro-Hydraulic** (Design #70) pump. This creates a "Zero-Grit" fluid bearing that is virtually indestructible and impervious to dust. The position is locked using "Magnetic Breakaway" clamps that interface with the Pixel 9a for precision celestial tracking.

## 3. Organic Form
The Gimbal looks like a "Polished Eye" or a "Stone Orb" resting in a silver-fumed socket. It is silent and requires zero petroleum lubricants. It is the "Joint" that allows the Walipa project to look upward with a "Steady Eye," maintaining its focus on the higher dimensions while staying grounded in the Earth's mineral reality.
