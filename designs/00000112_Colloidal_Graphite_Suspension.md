# Walipa Design #00000112: Colloidal Graphite Suspension
**Status:** Phase 2 (Conductive Media & Thermal Interface)

## 1. The Vision
This is the "Blood" of the GTC Forge. By suspending high-purity graphite in an aqueous silicate humor, we create a conductive "Ink" that can be painted onto basalt fibers or vitrified surfaces. It eliminates the "Grit" of heavy copper wiring by allowing the structure itself to carry the Phoebe Frequency.

## 2. Technical Logic
The suspension uses 99.9% pure micronized graphite mixed into a 10% solution of **Sodium Silicate** (Design #113). The mixture is agitated using the **Aqueous Vortex Stirrer** (Design #06) for 6 cycles. The result is a non-toxic, high-temp conductive coating that can withstand the heat of the Thermal Lance (#110).

## 3. Organic Form
The suspension is a deep, shimmering black liquid. When applied to the **Basalt Mesh** (#107), it creates a "Mirror-Shadow" finish that is both electrically active and visually stunning.
