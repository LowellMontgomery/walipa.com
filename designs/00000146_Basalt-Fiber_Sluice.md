# Walipa Design #00000146: Basalt-Fiber Sluice
**Status:** Phase 2 (Mineral Reclamation & Water Sculpting)

## 1. The Vision
Inspired by the historic mining of Nevada County, but reimagined for restoration. This sluice is used to reclaim mineral fines from mountain runoff or to separate **Graphite** from raw substrate. It turns the "Grit" of erosion into the building blocks of the Oasis.

## 2. Technical Logic
The sluice body is a continuous-form **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138) coated in **Sodium Silicate**. The riffles are designed using a **Laminar Flow** pattern to maximize the capture of heavy minerals while allowing organic matter to pass through to the **Myco-Filtration Casing** (#124).

## 3. Organic Form
A "Dark, Ribbed Stream-Bed" that looks like it grew from the earth. It sits in the drainage lines of the Nevada City slope, silently gathering the "Stored Light" of the minerals.
