# Walipa Design #00000153: Mycelial Humidity Sensor
**Status:** Phase 2 (Biological Atmospheric Monitoring)

## 1. The Vision
Electronic sensors often fail or drift in the extreme humidity of a **Subterranean Solarium** (#45). This design utilizes the mycelium's inherent sensitivity to atmospheric moisture. It is a "Living Hygrometer" that speaks directly to the **Phoebe Bio-Link** (#92), providing real-time biological feedback for the ecosystem.

## 2. Technical Logic
A small patch of *Trametes versicolor* (Turkey Tail) is cultivated between two **Fumed-Silver** (#117) graphite electrodes. As the relative humidity increases, the mycelium’s internal conductivity shifts in a predictable curve. The **Aqueous Logic Gate** (#123) interprets this resistance change as a data pulse. Unlike silicon sensors, this biological interface is self-healing and thrives in the very environment it monitors.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Velvety White Button" mounted on a dark basalt shard. It is a "Done State" for environmental sensing—non-toxic, indestructible through traditional wear, and perfectly attuned to the life cycles of the Walipa Foundation.
