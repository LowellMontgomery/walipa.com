# Walipa Design #00000157: Aqueous Pressure Regulator
**Status:** Phase 2 (Pneumatic System Safety)

## 1. The Vision
To manage the high pressures of the **Hydrogen Electrolysis Cell** (#143) and the **Ark Blimp Ballast**, we need a regulator that won't corrode, drift, or fail in the high Sierra environment. This design utilizes the "Grace" of gravity and water to maintain safety, replacing the "Grit" of mechanical spring-valves with a "Done State" of hydrostatic balance.

## 2. Technical Logic
The regulator utilizes a "Weight-on-Piston" system housed in a **Vitrified Basalt** cylinder. The weight of a vertical column of **Aqueous Humor** (#105) or a precision-cast basalt slug determines the release threshold. When gas pressure exceeds this threshold, a **Sodium Silicate**-coated valve lifts, venting excess pressure back into the **Myco-Filtration Casing** (#124). This ensures that the **ETFE Pressure Pods** (#130) never reach a failure point.

## 3. Organic Form
The regulator appears as a "Silver and Stone Pillar" standing guard over the pressure vessels. It is a "Passive Guardian," functioning entirely without electronics to ensure the "Healthy Innocence" of the high-pressure energy systems.
