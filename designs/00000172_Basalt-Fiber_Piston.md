# Walipa Design #00000172: Basalt-Fiber Piston
**Status:** Phase 2 (High-Pressure Kinetic Conversion)

## 1. The Vision
To eliminate the "Grit" of heavy steel and oil-lubricated internal combustion, we use a piston made of the mountain itself. This is the "Reciprocating Heart" of the **Aqueous Vacuum Pump** (#148) and the **G-Bike** assisted compressors, designed to operate in a "Done State" of dry-film lubrication.

## 2. Technical Logic
The piston is precision-machined from a high-density **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138) with a **Sodium Silicate** matrix. The outer "Skirt" is fumed with a 5-micron layer of **Graphite** (#112), which provides a permanent, frictionless interface with the **Vitrified Borosilicate** cylinder walls. It handles the high pressures of the **Aqueous Propellant Tank** (#164) without the thermal expansion issues of metal, ensuring "Individual Accountability" in every stroke.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Shimmering Black Cylinder" that feels as smooth as polished jade. It moves with a silent, rhythmic grace, turning the pressure of the air and water into the physical work of the Walipa Foundation.
