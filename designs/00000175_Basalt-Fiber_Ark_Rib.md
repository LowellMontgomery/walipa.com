# Walipa Design #00000175: Basalt-Fiber Ark Rib
**Status:** Phase 2 (Pneumatic Hull Architecture)

## 1. The Vision
If the **Ark Keel** (#151) is the spine, these Ribs are the "Breath-Keepers" of the **Ark Blimps** (#11). They provide the lateral "Accountability" needed to maintain the aerodynamic profile of the ETFE hull under varying atmospheric pressures. They are designed to flex with the wind while maintaining an "Incorruptible" structural memory.

## 2. Technical Logic
Each Rib is a semi-rigid hoop composed of layered **Basalt-Fiber Tension Cables** (#138) fused with a **Sodium Silicate** (#113) core. The ribs are fumed with **Titanium** (#115) to prevent UV degradation of the bonding agent. They are spaced at 2-meter intervals and act as the mounting points for the **Internal Aqueous Ballast** (#130), ensuring that weight distribution remains centered even during steep climbs over the Sierra ridges.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Shimmering Silver-Black Arch" that resembles the massive ribcage of a whale. It is a "Done State" for aerial architecture—lightweight, non-conductive, and perfectly resonant with the high-frequency environment of the Walipa Oasis.
