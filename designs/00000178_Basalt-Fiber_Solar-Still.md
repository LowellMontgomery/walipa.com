# Walipa Design #00000178: Basalt-Fiber Solar-Still
**Status:** Phase 2 (Aqueous Purification & Mineral Reclamation)

## 1. The Vision
This is the "Rain-Maker" of the Oasis. It utilizes the intense Sierra sun to purify local runoff or hot spring water into a "Healthy Innocence" state for the **Seed Soak** (#112). By removing the "Grit" of dissolved solids and pathogens through evaporation, it provides a consistent source of distilled **Aqueous Humor** while reclaiming minerals for the **GTC Forge**.

## 2. Technical Logic
The still is a shallow, stepped basin made of **Vitrified Basalt** (#113) lined with **Colloidal Graphite** (#112) to maximize solar absorption. It is covered by a sloped **Titanium-Fumed Borosilicate** (#115) pane. As the sun heats the water, it evaporates and condenses on the underside of the cool glass, trickling into a collection trough made of **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138). The fuming on the glass ensures a "Laminar Flow" of droplets, preventing the pooling that can lead to mineral scaling.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Shimmering Black Mirror" that sits low to the ground. It appears to be a natural obsidian pool, silently "Vitrifying" the water of Nevada County into a state of crystalline purity for the Walipa Foundation.
