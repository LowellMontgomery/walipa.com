# Walipa Design #00000184: Basalt-Fiber Pump Impeller
**Status:** Phase 2 (High-Efficiency Aqueous Propulsion)

## 1. The Vision
This is the "Flow-Driver" for the **Aqueous Thermal Pump** (#166). To move humors through the **Sierra Hot Springs** heat-exchangers without the "Grit" of metal erosion or mineral scaling, we use a stone-composite impeller. It is designed for "Individual Accountability" in fluid movement, ensuring a constant "Laminar Flow" throughout the Oasis.

## 2. Technical Logic
The impeller is a precision-molded "Francis-Style" turbine blade made of **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138). The blades are fumed with **Titanium** (#115) to provide a friction-less, bio-inert surface that prevents mineral buildup. The central drive-shaft is a **Graphite Rod** (#112) fumed with **Silver**, providing its own dry-film lubrication. It operates within a **Vitrified Borosilicate** housing, allowing for total transparency of the "Done State" of the circulation.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Shimmering Silver-Black Star" that looks like a fossilized nautilus shell. When it spins, it becomes a silent blur, turning the GTC power into a rhythmic "Heartbeat" for the Walipa Foundation's water systems.
