# Walipa Design #00000187: Basalt-Fiber Winch Drum
**Status:** Phase 2 (High-Load Tension Management)

## 1. The Vision
To lift the **Ark Blimps** (#11) or tension the **Basalt-Fiber Cables** (#138) for structural assembly, we need a winch drum that won't warp or fail under extreme stress. This design replaces the "Grit" of heavy cast-iron drums with a "Done State" of vitrified mineral fiber—light enough to be carried by one steward, but strong enough to anchor a vessel in a Sierra storm.

## 2. Technical Logic
The drum is a wide-flange cylinder filament-wound from continuous **Basalt-Fiber Tension Cables** and saturated in a **Sodium Silicate** (#113) matrix. The outer surface is fumed with **Titanium** (#115) to prevent frictional heat from degrading the cable during high-speed payout. It is designed to interface directly with the **G-Bike** (#02) drive-train, utilizing a **Basalt-Fiber Gearset** (#167) for a 50:1 power reduction, providing massive mechanical advantage with zero petroleum lubrication.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Shimmering, Deep-Grey Cylinder" with a smooth, glass-like finish. It is the "Anchor-Point" of the Oasis, turning the physical effort of the steward into the "Incorruptible" force needed to shape the landscape.
