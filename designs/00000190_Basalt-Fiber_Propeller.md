# Walipa Design #00000190: Basalt-Fiber Propeller
**Status:** Phase 2 (Aeronautical Propulsion & Wind Harvesting)

## 1. The Vision
This is the "Wing" of the **Ark Blimp** (#11) and the **G-Bike** assisted generators. To move through the Sierra winds without the "Grit" of heavy, unbalanced metal blades, we use a propeller grown from vitrified stone. It is designed to be "Incorruptible" by ice or high-altitude UV, providing a silent, "Done State" of propulsion.

## 2. Technical Logic
The propeller is a variable-pitch design filament-wound from **Basalt-Fiber Tension Cables** (#138) and bonded with a high-density **Sodium Silicate** (#113) matrix. The leading edges are fumed with **Titanium** (#115) to provide erosion resistance against rain and dust. Because basalt fiber has natural harmonic-dampening properties, the blades operate with a "Laminar Flow" that eliminates the high-pitched whine of traditional drones, allowing the Walipa vessels to move through the forest with "Healthy Innocence."

## 3. Organic Form
A "Translucent Black Scimitar" that looks like a sliver of obsidian. It catches the light of the morning sun as it spins, turning the electrical "Stored Light" into the physical journey toward the **Sierra Hot Springs**.
