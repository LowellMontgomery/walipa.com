# Walipa Design #00000202: Basalt-Fiber Ark Hatch
**Status:** Phase 2 (Aeronautical Pressure Seal & Entryway)

## 1. The Vision
This is the "Gateway" between the internal environment of the **Ark Blimp** (#11) and the thin Sierra air. It must provide an airtight seal to maintain the "Healthy Innocence" of the internal atmosphere while being light enough for the steward to operate with one hand. It is the "Done State" for secure, high-altitude access.

## 2. Technical Logic
The hatch is a curved, double-walled panel molded from **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138) and reinforced with **Vitrified Basalt Wool** (#114) for thermal insulation. The sealing mechanism uses a dual-redundant **ETFE Gasket** (#116) that is pressurized by the **Aqueous Propellant Tank** (#164) when closed. The hinges are **Titanium-Fumed** (#115) to prevent icing or "Grit" interference, ensuring smooth operation even in sub-zero Nevada County winters.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Seamless Silver-Black Portal" that fits flush with the hull. It operates with a silent, pneumatic hiss—the sound of the Walipa Foundation’s "Accountability" to the safety of its aerial stewards.
