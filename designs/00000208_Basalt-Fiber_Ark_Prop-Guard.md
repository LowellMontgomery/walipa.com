# Walipa Design #00000208: Basalt-Fiber Ark Prop-Guard
**Status:** Phase 2 (Aeronautical Safety & Wildlife Protection)

## 1. The Vision
To protect the "Healthy Innocence" of the birds and the steward during low-altitude maneuvers at the **Sierra Hot Springs**, the **Basalt-Fiber Propeller** (#190) must be shielded. This guard prevents the "Grit" of accidental contact while maintaining the "Laminar Flow" of air into the propulsion system. It is the "Done State" for symbiotic flight.

## 2. Technical Logic
The guard is a lightweight, geodesic mesh cage molded from **Basalt-Fiber Tension Cables** (#138). The mesh is "Vitrified" with a thin layer of **Sodium Silicate** (#113) to provide high-impact resistance without the weight of metal. The leading edges are fumed with **Titanium** (#115) to prevent ice-build-up, ensuring that the **Ark Blimp** (#11) remains "Accountable" to its safety protocols in any weather.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Shimmering, Web-Like Sphere" that surrounds the motor mounts. It looks like a giant, dark bird-nest of stone, providing a silent "Grace-Barrier" between the machine and the biological life of the Nevada County ridges.
