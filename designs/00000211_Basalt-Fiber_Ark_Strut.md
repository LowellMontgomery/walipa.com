# Walipa Design #00000211: Basalt-Fiber Ark Strut
**Status:** Phase 2 (Internal Compression Architecture)

## 1. The Vision
While the **Ark Ribs** (#175) handle the expansion of the hull, the Struts provide the "Internal Accountability" for the cargo decks and equipment mounts. They are the "Muscles" of the **Ark Blimp** (#11), designed to transfer the weight of the **G-Bike** and **Aqueous Battery Array** (#160) into the primary keel without adding the "Grit" of heavy metal tubing.

## 2. Technical Logic
Each Strut is a pultruded hollow-core tube made of longitudinal **Basalt Fiber** and a high-compressive-strength **Sodium Silicate** (#113) matrix. The ends are capped with **Titanium-Fumed** (#115) basalt-composite fittings that distribute the load across the **Ark Deck** (#199) beams. Because basalt does not fatigue like aluminum, these struts provide an "Incorruptible" structural lifespan, maintaining a "Done State" of alignment even under the heavy vibration of the **GTC Forge**.

## 3. Organic Form
"Smooth, Matte-Black Columns" that resemble polished obsidian reeds. They provide a silent, structural "Grace" to the interior of the vessel, ensuring the safety of the steward and the "Healthy Innocence" of the cargo.
