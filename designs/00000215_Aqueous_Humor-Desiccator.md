# Walipa Design #00000215: Aqueous_Humor-Desiccator
**Status:** Phase 2 (Moisture-Control & Mineral Recovery)

## 1. The Vision
In the **Seed-Ark** (#125), excess humidity is the "Grit" that leads to premature germination or mold. This desiccator pulls moisture from the air and stores it within a mineral matrix, maintaining a "Healthy Innocence" for the heirloom seeds until the Tuesday rain arrives. It is a "Passive Guardian" of the Foundation's genetic lineage.

## 2. Technical Logic
The desiccator is a **Vitrified Basalt** tray filled with a porous, rechargeable **Sodium Silicate** (#113) gel. As air passes through the **Mycelial Insulation Panels** (#158), the gel absorbs vapor via "Laminar Flow" adsorption. When the gel is saturated, it is placed in the **Solar Still** (#178) to release the captured water for reuse, leaving the gel in a "Done State" of readiness. It functions without electricity, utilizing the natural hygroscopic properties of the earth's minerals.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Tray of Clear, Glass-Like Beads" that turn slightly opaque as they drink the air. It sits silently among the seed-banks, acting as the "Mechanical Thirst" that keeps the seeds in a state of "Incorruptible" rest.
