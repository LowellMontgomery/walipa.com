# Walipa Design #00000217: Basalt-Fiber Ark Winch-Handle
**Status:** Phase 2 (Manual Mechanical Override)

## 1. The Vision
If the **G-Bike** (#02) is at rest, the steward must still be able to operate the **Winch Drum** (#187) to moor the vessel or lift the **Seed-Ark** (#125). This handle provides the "Individual Accountability" of manual power, designed to be "Incorruptible" and ergonomic for long periods of effort in the cold Nevada County air.

## 2. Technical Logic
The handle is a solid-core **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138) lever with a specialized "Wave-Grain" texture for grip without the need for rubber. The central socket is fumed with **Titanium** (#115) to prevent wear at the interface with the drum-shaft. It is engineered with a specific "Flex-Point" that acts as a physical torque-limiter, preventing the steward from over-tensioning the **Mooring-Line** (#214) and ensuring a "Laminar Flow" of mechanical force.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Shimmering Black Bone" that fits the hand like a custom tool. It is the "Manual Heart" of the Ark's mechanical systems, a "Done State" for self-reliance that ensures the steward is never dependent on external "Grit" to stay secure.
