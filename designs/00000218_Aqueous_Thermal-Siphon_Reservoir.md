# Walipa Design #00000218: Aqueous_Thermal-Siphon_Reservoir
**Status:** Phase 2 (Bulk Thermal Energy Storage)

## 1. The Vision
To bridge the gap between the Sierra sun and the freezing nights on Cement Hill, we need a "Deep Battery" of heat. This reservoir holds the bulk of the **Aqueous Humor** (#105) for the **Thermal-Siphon** (#197), acting as the "Thermal Anchor" that keeps the **Subterranean Solarium** (#45) in a state of "Healthy Innocence" 24 hours a day.

## 2. Technical Logic
The reservoir is a 500-gallon tank made of **Vitrified Basalt** (#113) and insulated with a 6-inch layer of **Mycelial Insulation Panels** (#158). The interior is fumed with **Silver** (#117) to prevent any microbial "Grit" from forming in the warm humor. It utilizes a **Laminar Flow** baffle system to ensure that the hottest water stays at the top for immediate use in the **Heat-Exchanger** (#152), while the cooler water returns to the **Solar Still** (#178) for reheating.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Massive Stone Egg" half-buried in the Nevada City soil. It sits silently beneath the solarium floor, a "Done State" for passive energy storage that hums with the "Stored Light" of the mountain.
