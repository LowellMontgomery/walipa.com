# Walipa Design #00000220: Basalt-Fiber Ark Deck-Cleat
**Status:** Phase 2 (Heavy-Duty Load-Point & Mooring Interface)

## 1. The Vision
The "Anchor-Point" of the interior. To secure the **G-Bike Utility** (#01) or the **Seed-Ark** (#125) during high-altitude turbulence, we need a cleat that is integrated into the very fiber of the **Ark Deck** (#199). It replaces the "Grit" of bolted metal with a "Done State" of vitrified structural integrity.

## 2. Technical Logic
The cleat is a "Horn-Style" fitting molded from high-density **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138). It is not bolted through the deck; instead, the fibers of the cleat are interwoven with the deck-skins during the resin-infusion process, creating a monolithic bond. The horns are fumed with **Titanium** (#115) to prevent friction-burn from the **Mooring-Line** (#214). It provides "Individual Accountability" for every piece of cargo, ensuring the "Laminar Flow" of the flight is never disrupted by shifting weight.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Smooth, Black Obsidian Hook" that rises naturally from the silver floor. It looks like a polished stone wave, providing a silent, "Incorruptible" grip that holds the Walipa Foundation together in the sky.
