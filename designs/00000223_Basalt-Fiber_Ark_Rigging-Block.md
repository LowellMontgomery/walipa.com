# Walipa Design #00000223: Basalt-Fiber Ark Rigging-Block
**Status:** Phase 2 (Low-Friction Mechanical Advantage)

## 1. The Vision
To manage the **Mooring-Lines** (#214) and internal cargo lifts without the "Grit" of heavy, squeaking metal pulleys. This block provides a "Done State" of silent mechanical advantage, utilizing the self-lubricating properties of vitrified minerals to ensure the steward can adjust the **Ark Blimp** (#11) with "Individual Accountability."

## 2. Technical Logic
The block housing is molded from high-density **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138). The internal sheave is a **Vitrified Borosilicate** wheel fumed with a 10-micron layer of **Graphite** (#112). This creates a permanent dry-film lubricant interface with the **Titanium-Fumed** (#115) axle. It handles the high-tension loads of the Sierra winds without heat-buildup or material fatigue, maintaining a "Laminar Flow" of kinetic energy through the rigging system.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Smooth, Obsidian Egg" with a silver-lined center. It sits silently in the rigging, looking like a water-worn stone from a Nevada County creek, providing the "Incorruptible" strength needed to harness the wind.
