# Walipa Design #00000230: Aqueous_Humor-Desiccator_Tray
**Status:** Phase 2 (Passive Humidity-Capture Architecture)

## 1. The Vision
This is the "Thirsty Stone" that lives within the **Seed-Ark** (#125). By utilizing the natural capillary action of vitrified minerals, this tray pulls the "Grit" of excess moisture from the air, turning it into a "Laminar Flow" of reclaimed water. it ensures the heirloom seeds remain in a state of "Incorruptible" dormancy until the Tuesday rain.

## 2. Technical Logic
The tray is a wide-format basin made of porous **Vitrified Basalt Wool** (#114) infused with a **Sodium Silicate** (#113) gel. It is designed to maximize surface area contact with the internal atmosphere of the solarium. As the gel absorbs moisture, its weight increases, triggering a **Piezo-Basalt Sensor** (#118) that alerts the **Bio-Link Hub** (#162) when the tray is "Done" and ready for solar-regeneration in the **Solar Still** (#178).

## 3. Organic Form
A "Leathery Black Basin" that appears to glisten with internal dew. It sits quietly at the base of the seed-racks, acting as the "Passive Kidney" of the Foundation’s biological storage systems.
