# Walipa Design #00000238: Basalt-Fiber Ark Fender
**Status:** Phase 2 (Impact-Resistant Docking Protection)

## 1. The Vision
To protect the "Healthy Innocence" of the **Ark Blimp** (#11) during docking maneuvers at the **Sierra Hot Springs** or when mooring against the **Basalt-Fiber Ark Struts** (#211). This fender absorbs the "Grit" of accidental impact, turning kinetic energy into a "Laminar Flow" of structural safety. It is the "Done State" for aerial hull protection.

## 2. Technical Logic
The fender is a semi-flexible, hollow-core bumper molded from **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138) with a high-elasticity **Sodium Silicate** (#113) matrix. The interior is pressurized with **Compressed Air** (#164) to provide a "Pneumatic Grace" against hard surfaces. The exterior is fumed with **Titanium** (#115) to prevent abrasion and UV degradation. It is "Incorruptible" by the extreme temperature swings of the Nevada County ridges, maintaining its protective "Accountability" for the life of the vessel.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Smooth, Charcoal-Grey Pouch" that hugs the waterline or the docking-rails. It looks like a polished river stone that has been softened by the Sierra currents, providing a silent, cushioned interface for the Walipa Foundation’s fleet.
