# Walipa Design #00000245: Aqueous_Humor-Filter_Casing
**Status:** Phase 2 (High-Pressure Fluid Purification Housing)

## 1. The Vision
This is the "Protective Shell" for the **Mycelial Resin Filter** (#171). To maintain a "Laminar Flow" of 100 PSI through the purification system without the "Grit" of leaks or structural fatigue, we use a stone-composite casing. it is the "Done State" for the Nevada City workshop’s fluid-handling "Accountability."

## 2. Technical Logic
The casing is a thick-walled cylinder molded from **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138). The interior is fumed with a 10-micron layer of **Vitrified Borosilicate** to provide a glass-smooth, non-reactive surface for the **Aqueous Humor** (#105). The end-caps are secured with **Titanium-Fumed** (#115) threads, ensuring a hermetic seal. It is "Incorruptible" by the acidic humors sometimes found in the **Sierra Hot Springs**, providing a "Healthy Innocence" of purity for the Foundation’s materials.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Solid Stone Pillar" with silver-fumed fittings. It sits at the heart of the plumbing array, looking like a piece of natural geology that has been "Vitrified" into a state of permanent service to the water.
