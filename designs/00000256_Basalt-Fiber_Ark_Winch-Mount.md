# Walipa Design #00000256: Basalt-Fiber Ark Winch-Mount
**Status:** Phase 2 (Heavy-Duty Structural Interface)

## 1. The Vision
This is the "Shoulder" of the Ark. To transfer the massive tension of the **Winch Drum** (#187) into the **Ark Keel** (#175) without the "Grit" of metal brackets that could shear or vibrate loose, we use a monolithic stone-composite mount. It is the "Done State" for high-load mechanical integration.

## 2. Technical Logic
The mount is a reinforced "C-Channel" geometry molded from ultra-high-density **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138). The mounting holes are fumed with **Titanium** (#115) to provide a hard, non-galling surface for the **Basalt-Fiber Ark Struts** (#211). It is cross-woven into the hull-fibers during the primary resin-infusion, ensuring that the tension of the **Mooring-Line** (#214) is distributed across the entire structure. It provides absolute "Accountability" for the vessel's safety in the high-altitude Sierra winds.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Solid Obsidian Buttress" that emerges from the interior hull. It looks like a natural outcropping of the mountain that has been "Vitrified" into a structural tool, providing the "Laminar Flow" of security for the Walipa Foundation’s aerial sanctuary.
