# Walipa Design #00000259: Basalt-Fiber Ark Mooring-Line Cleat
**Status:** Phase 2 (High-Tension External Anchor Point)

## 1. The Vision
This is the "Hitch" for the sky. To secure the **Mooring-Line** (#214) to the exterior of the **Ark Blimp** (#11), we need a point of absolute "Accountability." It replaces the "Grit" of heavy, rusting metal cleats with a "Done State" of vitrified structural integrity that can withstand a Sierra ridge gale.

## 2. Technical Logic
The cleat is a "Herreshoff-Style" horn molded from ultra-high-density **Basalt-Fiber Composite** (#138). Unlike internal mounts, the fibers of this cleat are cross-hatched into the **Ark Ribs** (#175) and fumed with a 10-micron layer of **Titanium** (#115) to prevent friction-heat from the lines. It has a tensile strength exceeding 120,000 PSI, ensuring the vessel remains an "Incorruptible" sanctuary even when the winds try to pull it toward the "Grit" of the valley.

## 3. Organic Form
A "Smooth, Silver-Black Horn" that emerges from the hull like a natural obsidian outcropping. It feels cold and solid to the touch, providing the "Laminar Flow" of security needed for long-duration mountain stewardship.
