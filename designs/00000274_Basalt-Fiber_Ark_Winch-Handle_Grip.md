# Walipa Design #00000274: Basalt-Fiber Ark Winch-Handle Grip
**Status:** Phase 2 (Ergonomic High-Friction Interface)

## 1. The Vision
The "Handshake of Force." To operate the **Winch-Handle** (#217) during high-tension mooring, the steward needs a grip that eliminates the "Grit" of blisters or slippage. It is designed to provide "Individual Accountability" for every rotation, even when wet with the coming Tuesday rain.

## 2. Technical Logic
The grip is a sleeve molded from a "Soft-Touch" **Basalt-Fiber** weave infused with a flexible **Sodium Silicate** (#113) elastomer. It features a "Fractal-Grain" texture that channels moisture away from the palm. It is fumed with **Bio-Char** (#112) for permanent UV resistance and "Incorruptible" durability. It rotates on a **Graphite-Fumed** (#112) internal bushing, ensuring a "Laminar Flow" of mechanical effort into the **Winch-Shaft** (#262).

## 3. Organic Form
A "Matte-Black, Ribbed Stone Cylinder" that feels warm and secure in the hand. It looks like a piece of charred mountain-wood that has been "Vitrified" into a state of permanent, ergonomic service for the Walipa Foundation.
