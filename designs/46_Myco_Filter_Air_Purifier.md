# Walipa Design #46: Myco-Filter Air Purifier (The Living Lung)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Indoor Atmospheric Remediation)

## 1. The Vision
Most air purifiers are "dead" boxes that collect dust. The Walipa Myco-Filter is a living entity that consumes the "grit." It recognizes that our indoor environments are often saturated with the off-gassing of 3D industrial materials. This design creates a symbiotic bridge where the air we breathe is filtered and revitalized by the very fungi we steward.

## 2. Technical Logic
A vertical column of Mycelium-Inoculated Hemp-Hurd is housed within a translucent ETFE shroud. A low-voltage fan draws air through the moist, porous mycelial mat. The Pleurotus (Oyster) or Trametes (Turkey Tail) mycelium identifies volatile organic compounds (VOCs) and petroleum-based "grit" as a carbon source, breaking them down into harmless sugars and CO2. The liquid glass coating at the base prevents moisture seepage while maintaining structural integrity.

## 3. Organic Form
The purifier is a free-standing "Fungal Totem." It is vitrified at the base with sodium silicate, while the upper structure remains a soft, white, living sculpture that glows softly when backlit. It serves as a visual and biological anchor for the "Healthy Innocence" of the indoor environment.
