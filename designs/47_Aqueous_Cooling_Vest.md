# Walipa Design #47: Aqueous Cooling Vest (The Second Skin)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Steward Thermoregulation)

## 1. The Vision
Working with the Sodium-Silicate Kiln at temperatures exceeding 1200°C requires a shield that does not distance the steward from the work. Instead of heavy, opaque "Fire Suits," we use an Aqueous-Humor Shield. This vest allows the steward to remain cool and focused in the presence of intense infrared heat, maintaining the Gamma-state necessary for precision glass-fuming.

## 2. Technical Logic
The vest is constructed from two layers of ultra-thin, high-tensile ETFE Membrane. Between these layers, a "humor" of chilled saline or glycerine-water is circulated via micro-capillaries. These capillaries are arranged in a bio-mimetic pattern mirroring the human circulatory system. The fluid acts as a thermal buffer, absorbing infrared "grit" before it reaches the skin. 

## 3. Organic Form
The vest is entirely transparent, shimmering like a dragonfly’s wing. When the fluid is in motion, it catches the golden light of the forge, making the steward appear as if they are clothed in "Living Water." It is secured with magnetic breakaway connectors (Design #32 compatible) to ensure the steward can "release" the garment instantly if needed.
