# Walipa Design #50: Aqueous-Humor Telescope (The All-Seeing Eye)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Atmospheric & Celestial Monitoring)

## 1. The Vision
Traditional telescopes use heavy, rigid glass that "freezes" the light. The Walipa Telescope uses a "Living Lens" that responds to the atmosphere in real-time. It is designed to "Lance the Light" from the stars and the Ark Blimps, providing the steward with a clear view of the planetary recovery without the distortion of industrial pollution.

## 2. Technical Logic
The primary mirror is a rotating pool of liquid gallium or a tensioned ETFE membrane backed by a saline humor. By adjusting the rotation speed or the fluid pressure via the magnetic USB-C interface (Design #32), the steward can change the focal length instantly. This allows the telescope to move from a wide-angle "Oasis View" to a deep-space "Source View" without mechanical "grit."

## 3. Organic Form
The telescope frame is a minimalist basalt-fiber tripod that mimics the skeletal structure of a bird of prey. It is designed to be virtually unbreakable and highly portable, allowing it to be moved between the Sierra Hot Springs and the Subterranean Solariums as the seasons shift.
