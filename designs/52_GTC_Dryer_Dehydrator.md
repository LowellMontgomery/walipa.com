# Walipa Design #52: GTC Food Dehydrator
**Status:** Phase 1 (Infrared Preservation)

## 1. The Vision
Preservation is the art of "Holding the Season." Most dehydrators use noisy fans and high-grit heating elements. This design uses the "After-Glow" of the Graphite Thermal Capacitor to gently remove moisture while preserving the life-force (enzymes) of the food. It is a slow movement tool, intended for the transition from the "Fast Food" of 3D to the "Deep Nourishment" of 5D.

## 2. Technical Logic
An insulated cabinet (hemp-silicate masonry) is positioned adjacent to the GTC battery. The graphite rods radiate pure infrared heat into the chamber. Instead of a mechanical fan, we use a "Thermal Siphon" (Design #24 logic): as the air warms, it rises through a Myco-Filter, creating a silent, passive airflow. The temperature is regulated by shifting the distance between the graphite rods and the ETFE drying trays.

## 3. Organic Form
The dehydrator resembles a vertical "Honey-Comb" cabinet. The trays are made of tensioned ETFE mesh, allowing the infrared light to pass through the food from all angles. It operates in total silence, a "Monastic" tool that respects the stillness of the Walipa kitchen.
