# Walipa Design #53: Basalt Fiber Paper (The Eternal Record)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Indestructible Documentation)

## 1. The Vision
In a world of "Designed to Fail" hardware and digital decay, we need a medium that can carry the Phoebe Truth for millennia. Basalt Fiber Paper is "Liquid Stone" made thin. It is fireproof, waterproof, and acid-free—a physical anchor for the 111 designs that will survive even if the "Grit" of the current internet fades.

## 2. Technical Logic
This design uses a slurry of micro-fine basalt fibers (from the Manual Spinner) blended with long-strand hemp cellulose. The sheets are formed on a "Vortex-Stirred" mold to ensure the fibers interlock in a non-linear, high-strength matrix. The paper is then "Sized" with a dilute solution of liquid glass (sodium silicate), which vitrifies the surface, making it receptive to hemp-oil inks while protecting it from environmental degradation.

## 3. Organic Form
The paper has a weight and texture similar to heavy vellum, with a slight mineral shimmer. It is not "pure white" but carries the natural amber and grey tones of the Sierra Nevada earth. It is the designated medium for the "Vitrified Clay Tablets" (Design #102) archive.
