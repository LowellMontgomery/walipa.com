# Walipa Design #60: Aqueous Light Diffuser
**Status:** Phase 1 (Interior Illumination & Frequency Tuning)

## 1. The Vision
Artificial 3D lighting is often "harsh" and flickers at frequencies that cause ocular stress. The Aqueous Diffuser mimics the way sunlight is filtered through a forest canopy or a deep pool of water. It "softens the light," removing the "grit" of glare and replacing it with a rhythmic, living glow that supports the Gamma-state in Walipa living spaces.

## 2. Technical Logic
The diffuser is a suspended ETFE bladder filled with a humor of distilled water and a trace of vegetable glycerine. Small, high-CRI LED "Sun-Bells" (Design #10) are positioned above the bladder. As the light passes through the humor, it is refracted and dispersed. By adding a fraction of a teaspoon of **Colloidal Graphite**, the light is slightly polarized, reducing blue-light strain and mimicking the "Golden Hour" spectrum.

## 3. Organic Form
The diffuser is shaped like a "Luminous Jellyfish" or a soft, hanging "Water-Drop." It is virtually weightless and can be moved between the Portable Shelters. Because it is a closed-loop fluid system, it also acts as a minor thermal mass, holding the warmth of the light source and radiating it gently back into the room.
