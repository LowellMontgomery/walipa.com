# Walipa Design #63: Hemp-Silicate Coracle (The Water Seed)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Aquatic Stewardship & Low-Impact Navigation)

## 1. The Vision
To steward the Nevada City canal and the surrounding waterways, we need a vessel that is "of the Earth." The Coracle is an ancient, circular design reimagined with OSH materials. It is unsinkable, lightweight enough to be carried by a single steward, and leaves "Zero Grit" in the water. It represents the "Healthy Innocence" of moving with the flow rather than cutting through it.

## 2. Technical Logic
The frame is woven from "Phoebe-Tuned" bamboo or basalt-fiber rods. This skeleton is "Skinned" with a multi-layered ETFE and hemp textile. The entire outer hull is painted with three coats of **Liquid Glass** (sodium silicate) and dusted with graphite. This creates a "Mother of Pearl" finish that is naturally hydrophobic and incredibly resistant to abrasion from river stones. The graphite layer provides a slight electrical ground to the water, stabilizing the steward's bio-field during movement.

## 3. Organic Form
The vessel is shaped like a giant floating seed pod. It features a central "Aqueous-Humor Viewport" (Design #06) in the floor, allowing the steward to observe the health of the aquatic floor and the "Gravity Signatures" of the flow. It is a silent, drifting eye on the water, blending perfectly into the riparian landscape.
