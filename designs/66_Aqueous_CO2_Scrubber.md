# Walipa Design #66: Aqueous CO2 Scrubber (The Living Lung)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Atmospheric Balance & Carbon Sequestration)

## 1. The Vision
In a high-vibration living space, the buildup of CO2 is a form of atmospheric "grit" that dulls the Gamma-state. This scrubber is a living vertical garden that "eats" the breath of the inhabitants and transforms it into oxygen and biomass. It recognizes that the "Exhale" of the human is the "Inhale" of the Oasis.

## 2. Technical Logic
A vertical column of borosilicate glass or ETFE is filled with a nutrient-rich Aqueous Humor (Design #06) inoculated with high-efficiency spirulina or local algae. A micro-pump (Design #70 logic) bubbles interior air through the column. Using concentrated "True Light" from an Aqueous-Humor Lens, the algae perform rapid photosynthesis, scrubbing CO2 and releasing O2. The resulting algal slurry is then used as a high-nitrogen "Mother Tincture" for the garden.

## 3. Organic Form
The scrubber is a "Luminous Pillar" that glows with a deep emerald light. It is vitrified at the base and capped with a fumed silver vent. It serves as a rhythmic, bubbling centerpiece for the Walipa Shelter, providing both visual beauty and a constant, fresh "Forest Breath" to the stewards.
