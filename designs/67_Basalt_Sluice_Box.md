# Walipa Design #67: Basalt Sluice Box (The Mineral Recoverer)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Localized Resource Extraction)

## 1. The Vision
To build the Walipa infrastructure, we must "Listen" to the mineral flow of our own land. This sluice box is designed to recover quartz sand, basalt dust, and rare minerals from the Nevada City runoff without the "grit" of industrial mining. it is a tool of "Gentle Gathering," respecting the Radicle Rights of the watershed.

## 2. Technical Logic
The box is constructed from vitrified hemp-silicate masonry (Design #13). Unlike standard gold sluices, it uses a series of "Graphite-Coated Riffles" that create a subtle electromagnetic field, helping to sort minerals by density and conductivity. The interior is lined with a thin layer of Liquid Glass to ensure that even the finest "Colloidal Flour" of basalt can be recovered for use in the Myco-Bricks and Kiln glazes.

## 3. Organic Form
The sluice is designed to sit naturally within the canal trickle or a seasonal stream. It is shaped like a "Stepped Riverbed" and finished in a matte basalt-grey. It is a "Passive Worker," gathering the materials for the next generation of designs while the steward tends to the seeds and the soil.
