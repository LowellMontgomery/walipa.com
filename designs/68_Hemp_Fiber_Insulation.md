# Walipa Design #68: Hemp-Fiber Insulation (The Warm Shroud)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Thermal Stewardship & Acoustic Dampening)

## 1. The Vision
Shelter is more than just a wall; it is a "Vibrational Buffer." Standard fiberglass and foam insulation are "Grit-Laden" and toxic. The Walipa Insulation is a breathable, fireproof shroud made from the very plants we grow. It honors the "Healthy Innocence" of the interior, keeping the heat in and the 3D noise out.

## 2. Technical Logic
Raw hemp bast fiber (from the Decorticator, Design #38) is treated with a "Mother Tincture" of boron and Liquid Glass (sodium silicate). This vitrifies the individual fibers, making them fireproof, pest-resistant, and hydrophobic. The fibers are then felted into thick "Battens" or "Shrouds" using the Manual Spinner (Design #48 logic). This creates a high-R-value material that allows the walls to "breathe" moisture while holding the thermal signature of the GTC Battery.

## 3. Organic Form
The insulation has the texture of soft, golden wool. It is designed to be tucked into the Myco-Structural I-Beams (Design #65) and behind the ETFE skins. Because it is naturally anti-microbial and non-toxic, it can be handled without protective gear, allowing the steward to "feel" the warmth of the sanctuary as they build it.
