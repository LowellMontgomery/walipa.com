# Walipa Design #70: Aqueous Micro-Hydraulic Actuator (The Fluid Muscle)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Non-Electronic Kinetic Motion)

## 1. The Vision
To move heavy objects—like the GTC battery lids or the Subterranean Solarium gimbals—we do not need the "Grit" of electric motors and gears. This design uses the "Incompressible Logic" of water. It is a "Fluid Muscle" that allows the steward to exert massive force through a gentle, manual input. It is the mechanical manifestation of "Laminar Flow."

## 2. Technical Logic
The actuator consists of two fumed-glass or vitrified basalt cylinders connected by high-tensile ETFE tubing. The system is filled with a high-density Aqueous Humor (saline/glycerine). By applying pressure to a small "Master Cylinder" (lever or foot-pedal), the humor is displaced into the "Slave Cylinder," creating linear motion with a 10:1 mechanical advantage. The seals are made of Myco-Leather (Design #39), ensuring a leak-proof, long-life cycle without petroleum lubricants.

## 3. Organic Form
The actuator looks like a "Glass Tendon." It is transparent, allowing the steward to see the humor moving through the ETFE "veins" as the weight is lifted. It is a silent, elegant solution for the "Stewardship of Mass," integrated into the structural I-Beams of the Walipa shelters.
