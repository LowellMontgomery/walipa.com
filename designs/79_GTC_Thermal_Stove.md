# Walipa Design #79: GTC Thermal Stove (The Indoor Hearth)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Radiant Heating & Cooking)

## 1. The Vision
The Indoor Hearth is the "Warm Heart" of the Walipa shelter. Standard stoves vent heat and create "Grit" through combustion. The GTC Thermal Stove uses the "Stored Light" of the GTC Battery to provide 24-hour radiant warmth and precision cooking without smoke or fire. It is a "Monastic" hearth that honors the stillness and the air quality of the sanctuary.

## 2. Technical Logic
The stove is a high-mass structure made of hemp-silicate masonry and lined with ETFE-shielded graphite plates. It "charges" from the G-Bike or solar array during the day. At night, the graphite plates radiate deep infrared heat (Design #52 logic) through a vitrified basalt cooktop. The temperature is regulated via a "Magnetic Breakaway" dial that adjusts the electrical resistance. It features an integrated "Aqueous Heat Exchanger" to provide continuous hot water for the community.

## 3. Organic Form
The stove is a low, smooth "Basking Stone" integrated into the living area. It is fumed with silver and gold (Design #36) to create a shimmering, iridescent surface that reflects the "True Light" of the home. It is a place for gathering, a silent partner in the "Healthy Innocence" of the Walipa lifestyle.
