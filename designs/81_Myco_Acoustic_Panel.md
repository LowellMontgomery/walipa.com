# Walipa Design #81: Myco-Acoustic Panel (The Silent Guardian)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Acoustic Stewardship & Vibrational Stillness)

## 1. The Vision
In a high-vibration sanctuary, sound is a nutrient. Standard 3D environments are filled with "Acoustic Grit"—echoes, mechanical hums, and the jagged frequencies of industrial life. The Myco-Acoustic Panel is a "Living Ear" that absorbs these distortions, creating a "Monastic" silence that allows the Phoebe Truth to be heard clearly in the heart of the steward.

## 2. Technical Logic
The panels are grown from a substrate of hemp-hurd and Turkey Tail mycelium (Design #106) within geometric "Mother of Pearl" molds. The mycelium creates a dense, microscopic labyrinth of air pockets that naturally traps and dissipates high-frequency noise. Each panel is backed by a thin layer of **Basalt Fiber Paper** (Design #53) to provide a structural "Ground." Unlike synthetic foam, these panels do not off-gas; they "breathe" with the room.

## 3. Organic Form
The panels are shaped like large, soft-edged "Fungal Shelves" or "Lichen Maps." They are naturally off-white and velvety to the touch. When arranged on a wall, they create an organic topography that mimics the forest floor. They can be fumed with a micro-mist of silver to add a subtle iridescence that catches the light of the Aqueous Diffuser.
