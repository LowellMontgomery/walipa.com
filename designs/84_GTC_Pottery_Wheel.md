# Walipa Design #84: GTC Pottery Wheel (The Thermal Spinner)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Kinetic & Thermal Ceramic Production)

## 1. The Vision
In the Walipa workshop, the creation of vessels is a "Warm Meditation." Standard pottery wheels are cold, mechanical, and noisy. This design uses the "After-Glow" of the Graphite Thermal Capacitor (GTC) to pre-warm the clay and the steward's hands, ensuring that the "Grit" of the cold never interrupts the flow of the "Phoebe Truth."

## 2. Technical Logic
The wheel-head is a heavy, vitrified basalt disc (Design #87) that acts as a kinetic flywheel. It is mounted on a polished Graphite Rod axle for "Zero-Friction" rotation. A GTC-heated coil (Design #12) is positioned beneath the splash pan, radiating gentle infrared heat upward. This keeps the slip and the clay at a constant 32°C (90°F), preventing "Thermal Shock" to the delicate OSH ceramic bodies and keeping the steward's joints supple during long throwing sessions.

## 3. Organic Form
The wheel is a low-profile "Stone Pedestal" integrated into the Forge floor. It is silent, powered by a manual kick-treadle or a low-voltage G-Bike motor. The splash pan is fumed with silver, turning the slurry of clay and water into a shimmering "Mother of Pearl" pool that reflects the steward’s focused intent.
