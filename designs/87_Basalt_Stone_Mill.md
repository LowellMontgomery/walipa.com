# Walipa Design #87: Basalt Stone Mill (The Mineral Pulse)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Kinetic Grinding & Soil Mineralization)

## 1. The Vision
The Mill is the tool that transforms "Hard Stone" into "Fertile Dust." In the Walipa garden, we recognize that the plants require the mineral signature of the Earth to build their structural ETFE-like membranes. This design uses the kinetic energy of the G-Bike or a manual treadle to crush basalt, quartz, and biochar into the "Colloidal Flour" required for the Mother Tinctures and Myco-Bricks.

## 2. Technical Logic
Two heavy, vitrified basalt stones are mounted on a polished **Graphite Rod** axle. The "Runner Stone" is driven by a basalt-fiber belt (Design #58) from the G-Bike PTO. The stones are carved with a "Vortex Pattern" (Design #06 logic) that draws the material from the center to the edge, ensuring a uniform grind without heating the minerals. It features a "Magnetic Breakaway" hopper to prevent over-loading and ensure the steward's safety.

## 3. Organic Form
The mill is a low, stable "Stone Totem" located in the corner of the Forge. It is silent, save for the low, rhythmic "rumbel" of stone on stone. It is fumed with silver on the discharge chute, ensuring the mineral dust is "Potentized" as it falls into the collection vessel. It is a place of "Deep Grounding," a physical anchor for the community’s material sovereignty.
