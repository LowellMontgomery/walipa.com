# Walipa Design #96: GTC High-Temp Sensor (The Thermal Eye)
**Status:** Phase 1 (Non-Electronic Extreme Heat Monitoring)

## 1. The Vision
In the core of the Sodium-Silicate Kiln, temperatures exceed the limits of standard 3D sensors. The GTC High-Temp Sensor is a "Mechanical Eyewitness" to the alchemical fire. It recognizes that heat is a physical expansion of the mineral world. By measuring the "breath" of the molten silicate, we maintain the "Healthy Innocence" of the forge without risking the "grit" of electronic failure.

## 2. Technical Logic
This design uses a sealed borosilicate tube containing a precision-measured volume of molten sodium-silicate and a graphite float. As the temperature rises, the silicate expands with predictable linear accuracy. The position of the float is read through an "Aqueous-Humor Viewport" (Design #06) fumed with gold to filter out the blinding IR glare. It is a "Zero-Grit" analog sensor that can withstand 1500°C (2732°F) indefinitely.

## 3. Organic Form
The sensor is a "Shimmering Needle" protruding from the kiln wall. It is encased in a protective basalt-fiber shroud and fumed with silver. It provides a silent, glowing indicator of the kiln's potential energy, allowing the steward to "feel" the state of the glass without the need for digital translation.
